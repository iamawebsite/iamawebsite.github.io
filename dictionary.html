In order to orient you (and myself) in the vocabulary terms I am borrowing from several philosophical heavyweights, I wrote up this dictionary. Words without bracketed authors are ones I use in relation to other concepts without specifically borrowing from anywhere. It began as a personal reference, but I wanted to share it with anyone for whom it is useful!

Re-ontologization: (verb) Radical change of what something is or can be. [Luciado Florini]

Potentiological:(adjective) Relating to the continuity of what can happen in an objects future given its circumstances, either physical or conceptual. Relates to re-ontologization.

Cyborg:(noun) Cybernetic fusion of the organic self with the artificial in a way that does not seek wholeness or end, productive of irony. [Donna Haraway]

Cybernetic: (adjective) Descriptive of processes that make organic and inorganic material into Haraway’s cyborg.

Flattening:(verb) The restriction of one’s self concept by its ability to fit into systems of identity definition, either by intentional repression or unintentional destruction of pathways to self discovery, both of which resign the subject’s “identity” into the ineffable. [Legacy Russell]

Glitch:(verb) The refusal to be flattened. (noun) A positive identification with the way one breaks systems of identity definition via their embodiment of multiplicitous or paradoxical categorizations. [Legacy Russell]

Narratology:(verb) The study of how meaning and motion is created in a text such that it is applicable to multiple texts. (noun) A specific theory of texts that follow a specific narrative structure.

Ekphrasis:(verb) In this text: Obsessive re-writing of one’s own self-image. Changes in what is represented are reflective of changes in how it is able to represent itself. [Tomás Laurenzo] Modified from colloquial use for a textual description of a work of art.

Representation apparatus:(noun) Analogous to the “body” in mind/body dualism, the structure where ideas play out in front of the self and potentially others. [Tomás Laurenzo]
